Sublimation Printing

Full-color, edge-to-edge, permanent prints that never crack, peel, or fade—here's how it works.

The Process

What is Dye Sublimation?

Dye sublimation is a digital printing process that uses heat to transfer ink onto polyester fabric. Unlike traditional printing methods that lay ink on top of material, sublimation ink is converted from a solid to a gas under high heat and pressure. The gaseous ink penetrates the polyester fibers and bonds at a molecular level, becoming part of the fabric itself.

The result is a permanent, full-color print that won't crack, peel, fade, or wash out—no matter how many times you wear or wash it. Because the ink becomes part of the fabric, you can't feel the print at all. The garment stays soft, breathable, and comfortable.

Sublimation Process
Step by Step

How It Works

1

Create Design

Your artwork is prepared digitally at full resolution using professional design software. Every color, gradient, and detail is mapped for edge-to-edge coverage.

2

Print on Transfer Paper

The design is printed onto special sublimation transfer paper using high-quality sublimation inks in CMYK color space.

3

Heat Press at 400°F

The transfer paper is placed on the polyester garment and pressed at 400°F (204°C) under high pressure for 45–60 seconds. The heat converts the solid ink into gas.

4

Permanent Bond with Fabric

The gaseous ink penetrates and bonds with the polyester fibers at a molecular level. Once cooled, the print is permanent—it is the fabric, not on the fabric.

Benefits

Why Sublimation?

Sublimation printing delivers advantages that no other printing method can match.

Full Color

Unlimited colors with photographic quality. Print complex gradients, photographs, and intricate designs with no extra cost per color.

Permanent

Won't crack, peel, fade, or wash out. The ink bonds with the fabric at a molecular level, making the print as durable as the garment itself.

Edge-to-Edge

Prints cover the entire garment from seam to seam. No limitations on print area—your design wraps around every panel of the garment.

Eco-Friendly

Water-based sublimation inks produce zero liquid waste. No screens, plates, or setup waste—making it one of the most sustainable printing methods available.
