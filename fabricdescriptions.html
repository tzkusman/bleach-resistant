Fabric Knowledge

Understanding Sublimation Fabrics

Dye sublimation requires 100% polyester fabric to work. During the heat press process, sublimation ink converts from a solid to a gas and permanently bonds with polyester fibers at a molecular level. This is why polyester content is critical—the higher the polyester percentage, the more vibrant and durable the print. Cotton and blended fabrics cannot absorb sublimation ink, resulting in faded or washed-out prints.

All of our garments are carefully selected for their polyester content, weave quality, and print performance. We test every fabric to ensure it delivers the rich, full-color results our customers expect.

Maintenance

Care Instructions

Sublimation prints are permanent—but proper care keeps your garments looking their best for years.

  • Machine wash cold (30°C / 86°F) on a gentle cycle
  • Turn garments inside out before washing
  • Use mild detergent—avoid bleach and fabric softener
  • Do not dry clean or iron directly on the print
  • Tumble dry low or hang dry for best results
  • Avoid prolonged direct sunlight when storing
  • Sublimation prints will not crack, peel, or fade with normal washing
